What is the treaty of waitangi?


The Treaty is an agreement, written in both Māori and English, to address various issues arising from the influx of British migrants to New Zealand in the late 1830s. These issues included large-scale land transactions with Māori, unruly behavior by some settlers, and concerns about French interest in annexing New Zealand. The British government eventually realized that annexing the country could protect Māori, regulate British subjects, and secure commercial interests.


who was involved?


he Treaty was signed on 6 February 1840 between the British Crown and approximately 540 Māori rangatira (chiefs) in the Bay of Islands.


Why was it made?


The Treaty of Waitangi was a constitutional document that established and guided the relationship between the British Crown and the Māori people,The Treaty aimed to protect Māori culture and rights235and to enable British settlement and sovereignty145. However, there were differences between the Māori and English versions of the Treaty, which caused conflict.
